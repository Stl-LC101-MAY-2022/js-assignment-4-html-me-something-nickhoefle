Yellowstone Trip - July 2008

The Trip

In 2008, I had the opportunity to visit Yellowstone Park. A last minute cancellation led to me joining a friend's family vacation. The trip was awe-inspiring. The landscape was full of breathtaking views, mountains, lakes, wildflowers, megafauna, and more. While I am not the most traveled person, it is far and away the most amazing place I have visited. Our mode of transportation was a Ford Explorer with an RV hitched to it, and we logged almost 3,000 miles in total. To anyone reading this: if you have not visited, you must!

Old Faithful

Yellowstone's most famous geyser. Erupts occur every 60 to 110 minutes and reach 100 to 180 feet.

Bison

The park is home to 2,300 to 5,500 of these hulking creatures. Yellowstone is the only place in the U.S. where bison have lived continuously since prehistoric times.

Mountains

Yellowstone is located in the Rocky Mountains. There are 70 named mountain peaks over 8,000 feet in Yellowstone.

Waterfalls

There are at least 45 named waterfalls in Yellowstone. Silver Cord Cascade is the highest at 1,200 feet.

Lodge

The lodge we stayed at for a couple of nights. Adjacent to Old Faithful and constructed in 1923.

Hot Springs

These springs are the most common geothermal feature in Yellowstone. The colors come from clusters of different microorganisms. Don't jump in - temperatures can reach up to 199 fahrenheit.
